The numbers are small — but the trajectory is not. Chinatown has survived urban renewal, highway construction, and Tufts Medical Center claiming a third of its land. Elders still gather to play mahjong by the gate. Hudson Street still lights up with red lanterns at Lunar New Year. The question is whether it can survive the arithmetic of the market.
